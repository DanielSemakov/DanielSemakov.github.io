Home

Chess is a board game played between two players on an 8x8 grid. Each player begins with 16 pieces, which include 8 pawns, 2 rooks, 2 knights, 2 bishops, a queen, and a king. The game originated in India during the 6th century but began to take on its modern form in the 15th century.

The elo rating system is used to determine the strength of a player. There are currently over 2000 Grandmasters, or players with at least 2500 elo and three grandmaster norms.

How Do You Play?

Chess is played on an 8x8 board. Two sides, white and black, take turns making moves until one player wins by placing their opponent in checkmate or the two players draw. The players draw once one player is put in stalemate (they can't make any moves) or neither player has enough pieces left to checkmate. During each turn, a player can only move one of their pieces.

Pieces in the game:

Pawn (1 point): moves up one square, or two squares if it is on the starting square
Knight (3 points): moves in an L-shape (two squares in one direction and one square in the perpendicular direction)
Bishop (3 points): moves any amount of squares diagonally
Queen (9 points): moves any amount of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally
King: moves one square horizontally, vertically, or diagonally

Why Should You Play?

You should play chess because it's a fun and engaging game which exercises your mind. From its simple, elegant rules spring forth a complex game that has perplexed people around the world for centuries. The game is at an especially exciting point right now because there is a new generation of players who are pushing toward the top of the leaderboard and challenging the reign of the older legends. In the upcoming 2024 World Chess Championship, Gukesh D. (18 years old) has the chance to take the throne from the reigning World Champion Ding Liren (32 years old).
